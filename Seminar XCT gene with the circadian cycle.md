### XCT
XCT encodes  a protein --- protein that is highly conserved across eukaryotes (yeast, plants, animals)
XCT is a nuclear-localized protein (expressed in many organs cell nuclear)
The protein sequence doesn't give us any clues about its biochemical function

Mutant plants: 
- don't make any functional XCT protein(xct-2)
- don't grow well (small plants, delayed greening, not much pollen, misshaped leaves)
DNA point mutation (a ***SNP*** )
	- disrupt  ***a splice site junction*** (xct-1) look pretty normal
	- make a mutant XCT protein lacks 3 amino acids
**Both** *xct-1* and *xct-2* mutant have a ***similar short-period*** (***fast clock***) phenotype.

## Plants without XCT (*xct-2*)
### Phenotypes:
- Clock runs fast
- General Growth problems (rosette size, chlorophyll content, hypocotyl length, flowering time)
- Enhanced resistance to infection by a virulent bacterial pathogen
- Hypersensitive to DNA-damaging treatments
- Problems with mRNA splicing (make mistakes when choosing 3' slice site)
	- *xct-1* plants also have splicing defects
	- but less severe than *xct-2* 
- Altered expression of thousands of genes
### XCT protein co-purifies with:
- Components of the spliceosome(=complex that splices RNA)
- Proteins that bind chromatin (= chromosome + protein)
